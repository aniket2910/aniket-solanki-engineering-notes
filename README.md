# 🚀 Aniket's Engineering Notes

### Hey there, fellow engineer. 

If you are reading this, chances are you are in the thick of the interview grind. I know exactly what you are going through. 

You’ve probably spent countless late nights grinding through endless LeetCode problems, sketching out System Design boxes, debugging JS closures, memorizing TS types, reviewing React reconciliation, and reading up on Node, Postgres, Databases, Kafka queues, and OS cores. It is an overwhelming mountain of information to carry in your head, and interview prep can feel incredibly isolating and exhausting. You are trying to balance work, life, and the high-pressure demand to perform flawlessly on a whiteboard. 

**If you’ve ever felt burned out, lost interest, or wondered if you're studying the right way—you have come to the right place.**

This repository is built to be a sanctuary from the noise. It’s where I lay out real engineering problems and break them down to their absolute core. But I don't know everything, and I make mistakes too. If you spot a bug or a typo, or if you have a cleaner way to solve something, please correct me! Follow the PR template, share your solutions, and let's make this a space where we learn and grow together.

To build this revision guide, I pair-programmed with the **Antigravity AI** tool. My goal was simple: strip away the dry, boring, and over-engineered academic jargon. Instead, I wanted to keep it simple, visual, and minimal—focusing on intuitive real-world analogies and clean TypeScript code. It's designed to help you quickly review, keep your momentum high, and actually enjoy the preparation process without losing interest.

I will be gradually covering all the major engineering topics here, so please stay tuned. 

In this incredible AI era, a huge thank you to the tools that make our daily engineering workflows easier and keep our creative sparks alive—like **Antigravity, ChatGPT, Claude, and GitHub Copilot**.

Take a deep breath. You’ve got this. Let's make this preparation journey a little more human.
